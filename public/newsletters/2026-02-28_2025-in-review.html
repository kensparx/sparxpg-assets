2025 in Review, And What's in the Mix for 2026

 

2025 was a big year at Sparx. We launched a new magazine, travelled to events across four provinces (and so many locally!) and leaned into partnerships that pushed our work into new territory. All of these activities have set the stage for an even more exciting 2026. 

 

Case in point: the second issue of Circular Economy Magazine is taking shape for spring; a new book from Dr. Victoria Hurth, Lorenzo Fioramonti, and Ben Renshaw are launching in North America in early March; and we've got a growing list of purpose-driven events on the calendar worth knowing about.

 

Keep scrolling for the recap of 2025, Circular Economy Magazine news, upcoming events, and social purpose resources.

Sparx’s 2025 Recap

Last year marked an important shift at Sparx. We leaned more heavily into our social purpose by launching content that convenes leadership conversations in the purpose-driven and circular economies.

 

From standing up Circular Economy Magazine, North America's only magazine dedicated to the circular economy, to our webinar series, Purpose Connections, as well as our time at 15+ events across British Columbia, Alberta, Ontario, and Québec, we helped catalyze the conversations that matter to building a better world for all.

 

Our 2025 recap covers the milestones, the partnerships, and the moments that made it a year worth documenting. Thank you to everyone who was part of it.

Featured Event: Beyond Profit Book Launch and Discussion




Beyond Profit: Purpose-Driven Leadership for a Wellbeing Economy, the new book by Dr. Victoria Hurth, Lorenzo Fioramonti, and Ben Renshaw, launches in North America in early March. To mark this important milestone, tune in on Monday, March 2 for a live discussion with the authors, moderated by Sophie Devonshire, CEO of The Marketing Society.


 


Expect a candid conversation around how purpose can rearchitect economies, organizations, and individuals alongside a global community of leaders.

Sparx readers will recognize the authors: Dr. Victoria Hurth and Lorenzo Fioramonti who served as guest editors of The Wellbeing Economy issue of Make The World Better Magazine. For a great primer on this launch discussion, watch the replay of our Purpose Connections session where the authors previewed the book's core ideas. This is the next chapter of that conversation.
